How much we take for granted

My journey to Tonle Sap Lake in Cambodia brought me to the fascinating Chong Kneas Floating Village. As I approached the village, I was immediately struck by the unique sight of houses perched on tall stilts, rising above the crocodile and snake-infested waters of the lake. It was a stark reminder of the challenging conditions that the locals endure on a daily basis.

While exploring the village, I encountered two young children, no more than five years old, with enormous snakes coiled around their necks. With an air of resilience beyond their years, they approached me, asking for a mere dollar in exchange for a photograph. It was a humbling moment that left a profound impact on me.
